# Observability in Software Engineering

Software engineering has grown immeasurably more complex in the past decade. From expanded legal requirements to the ongoing need to innovate and continuously improve their products, software companies face an increase in the amount as well as complexity of the code that they produce and maintain.

However, with every line of code comes the potential for bugs, errors and crashes that threaten the stability of the business. A single line of wrongly written code may take down the company's website, delete the database or expose private customer data to the public. 

Largest software outages are estimated to have financial impacts in the billions of dollars (CrowdSTRIKE), and software errors can ruin a company in under an hour (Knight Capital)
